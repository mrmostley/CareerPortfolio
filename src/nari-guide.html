NRI Money Guide

A simple, jargon-free guide to help you understand your options for keeping your hard-earned money safe, tax-free, and protected from currency fluctuations when moving it to India.

Last updated: March 2026 Focus: Dubai/UAE NRIs with Canara Bank Status: For awareness, not financial advice
Section 01

The Big Picture

Let's start with the most important thing to know:

The Golden Rule

Money you earn abroad as an NRI is NOT taxed in India when you bring it in through proper NRI bank accounts (NRE or FCNR). This is your legal right under the Income Tax Act and FEMA regulations. You are not "hiding" anything — this is exactly how the system is designed to work.

Why Dubai/UAE NRIs Have a Special Advantage

The UAE has zero personal income tax. India gives tax exemption on NRE/FCNR accounts. This means your savings can be genuinely tax-free on both sides — a rare and valuable position that NRIs in countries like the US or UK don't enjoy.

The Two Things to Protect Against

1. Indian Tax: As long as you use the right accounts (NRE/FCNR), your foreign earnings and interest are tax-free in India. The key is using the correct channels.

2. Rupee Depreciation: If you convert your AED/USD to INR today and the rupee weakens later, you've locked in a good rate. But if you plan to eventually take money back abroad, you might want it to stay in foreign currency. That's where FCNR comes in.

April 2026 Rule Change

New tax rules take effect from April 1, 2026. Indian citizens earning more than ₹15 lakh from Indian sources in tax-free countries like UAE will be treated as RNOR if they stay 120+ days. However, your Dubai salary and NRE/FCNR deposits remain tax-free. Still, be aware.

When NRE Makes Sense

You plan to settle in India and will need Rupees. You want higher interest rates (NRE FDs offer 6–7.5% vs ~4–5% in UAE). You're comfortable with the current AED-to-INR exchange rate. You want to invest in Indian mutual funds or property.

The Catch

Once your AED is converted to INR, you're exposed to currency risk. If the Rupee weakens against AED, your money is worth less in AED terms. If you ever need to send money back to Dubai, you get fewer Dirhams.

NRE FD Tip

Available as Savings, FD, RD, and Current accounts. Joint account only with another NRI — not with resident family. Can only be funded by foreign currency (wire transfers, FCY cheques, or transfers from NRE/FCNR).

A fixed deposit in India where your money stays in foreign currency (USD, GBP, EUR, CAD, AUD) — it does NOT get converted to Rupees. Think of it as a "dollar locker" inside an Indian bank.

When FCNR Makes Sense

You're unsure if you'll stay in Dubai, move elsewhere, or return to India. You want to protect savings from Rupee depreciation. You might need the money back in foreign currency someday.

The Catch

Interest rates are lower than NRE FDs (typically 3–5% for USD vs 6–7% for NRE). This is the price you pay for currency protection. Also, AED is not directly accepted — you convert AED to USD first (almost zero risk since AED is pegged to USD at ~3.67). Premature withdrawal before 1 year = zero interest.

If You Return to India

Your tax status depends on how many days you spend in India during a financial year (April 1 – March 31).

Timing Is Everything

Return after October of any year. This ensures you spend fewer than 182 days in that financial year, maintaining NRI status for one more year and extending your RNOR window. This can save you significant tax.

What to Do During RNOR Period

Let NRE FDs mature (interest still tax-free). Let FCNR deposits mature. Sell foreign assets if needed (gains tax-free during RNOR). Convert NRE to RFC account for dollar holdings. Inform your bank about the status change. Consult a CA before the RNOR window closes.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Not informing bank about status change → FEMA violation. Keeping NRE account active as Resident → penalties. Not declaring foreign assets after becoming full Resident → ₹10L penalty. Assuming you're still RNOR without checking → tax notice.
